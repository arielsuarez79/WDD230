Tucumán Chamber of Commerce

The first steps 1953 - 1963

In March 1946 the Chamber of Commerce and Industry of Tucumán was constituted, which brought together the main industrial and commercial entities of this Capital with influence in the rural departments. She was a precursor of business union management through congresses and meetings with representatives from all over northern Argentina. Tucumán was the first province to adapt to the new structure of the Argentine business union, with the Chamber of Commerce and Industry of Tucumán, made up of sub-chambers, beginning to build a second-degree entity. Movement that was consolidated when first-degree business associations decided to come together and call an assembly on February 26, 1953 to designate a Provisional Commission that later convened, for April 1, 1953, the General Constitutive Assembly of Tucumán, in which the Economic Federation of Tucumán was formed, whose first President was Eng. Aldo Capuano.
